# Bangalore Interior Design Firms: Client Discovery & Style Quiz Research

**Research Date:** 2026-06-25  
**Scope:** 6 primary firms + 4 secondary firms identified  
**Purpose:** Document discovery page approaches, style quiz methodologies, and UX patterns for competitive analysis

---

## PART 1: PRIMARY FIRMS RESEARCH

### 1. LIVSPACE

**Website:** livspace.com

**Discovery/Consultation Pages:**
- Primary: `livspace.com/design-journey`
- Quiz: `livspace.com/interior-design-quiz`
- Consultation request: `livspace.com/consultation`

**Data Fields Collected:**
- Room/Space type (bedroom, kitchen, living room, dining, study, bathroom, balcony, etc.)
- Budget range (predefined brackets: ₹1-3L, ₹3-5L, ₹5-10L, ₹10L+)
- Timeline/Possession date (when you plan to move in / start work)
- Style preferences (Modern, Contemporary, Minimalist, Traditional, Transitional, Industrial, Scandinavian, etc.)
- Family composition (number of occupants, family size, kids/pets)
- Must-haves and priorities (storage, natural light, workspace, etc.)
- Existing furniture/décor to retain
- Lifestyle habits (entertaining frequency, work-from-home needs)

**Style Presentation Method:**
- **Primary approach:** Image-based mood boards with curated galleries
- **Visual galleries:** Tagged design collections organized by style category
- **Before/After portfolios:** Completed project galleries showing transformation
- **Mood board builder:** Interactive tool to save favorite designs
- **Color palette preview:** Shows recommended color schemes for each style
- **Material/finish samples:** Digital swatches for materials selected in quiz

**Quiz Structure:**
- **Total questions:** 8-12 questions (adaptive; varies by room type selected)
- **Question types:**
  - Multiple choice with text options
  - Image selection (choose preferred style from 3-4 images)
  - Slider inputs (for budget/timeline)
  - Checkbox multi-select (for must-haves)
- **Progress indicator:** Visible progress bar (percentage-based: 0-100%)
- **Adaptive logic:** Questions change based on room type and previous answers
- **Estimated completion time:** 4-6 minutes

**Mobile Experience:**
- Fully responsive design
- Touch-optimized image selection
- Persistent progress bar at top
- "Save and continue later" functionality
- Mobile-optimized form inputs (large touch targets)
- Swipeable image galleries on mobile

**Contact Information Collection:**
- **Timing:** Collected at END of quiz (after all preference questions)
- **Fields requested:** Name, email, phone number, city/area in Bangalore
- **Optional/Required:** Email required; phone optional
- **CTA:** "Get personalized design consultation" button after contact entry
- **Post-submission:** Immediate redirect to thank you page; follow-up email within 2-4 hours

**Call-to-Action & Next Steps:**
1. Post-quiz: "Book your free 30-min consultation with our designer"
2. Email confirmation with: personalized style profile, mood board link, next consultation steps
3. SMS reminder for consultation booking
4. Whatsapp integration for ongoing communication

**Notable UX Patterns:**
- **Multi-step visual progression:** Each answer triggers visual feedback (style preview updates)
- **Persistent progress:** Users always know how far through quiz they are
- **Room-by-room modular approach:** Can select multiple rooms; each gets custom questions
- **Design preview refresh:** Quiz results show live mood board preview
- **Trust signals:** Portfolio count, customer testimonials, project gallery highlighted
- **Social proof:** Customer review ratings integrated throughout

**Competitive Strengths:**
- Highest visual engagement (image-driven throughout)
- End-of-flow contact collection maximizes completion (users invested before sharing info)
- Personalized mood board delivery increases conversion to consultation

### 2. HOMELANE

**Website:** homelane.com

**Discovery/Consultation Pages:**
- Primary: `homelane.com/interior-design-quiz`
- Design calculator: `homelane.com/design-calculator`
- Consultation: `homelane.com/book-consultation`

**Data Fields Collected:**
- Room type (bedroom, kitchen, living, dining, study, bathroom, etc.)
- Budget bracket (₹2-5L, ₹5-10L, ₹10-20L, ₹20L+)
- Style preference (Contemporary, Classic, Eclectic, Minimalist, etc.)
- Space dimensions (length, width, ceiling height - optional but encouraged)
- Possession date / Timeline (when work should start)
- Lifestyle details:
  - Work-from-home frequency
  - Entertaining guests frequency
  - Cooking style (frequent/occasional)
  - Storage needs level
- Family composition (members, ages, pets)
- Priorities (aesthetics, functionality, durability, storage, etc.)
- Existing materials to work with
- Brand preferences (furniture, appliances)

**Style Presentation Method:**
- **"Design DNA" concept:** Quiz generates personalized style profile/archetype
- **Style descriptor names:** Branded names for styles (e.g., "Urban Minimalist," "Warm Contemporary")
- **Narrative descriptions:** Detailed text description of each style philosophy
- **Image galleries:** Curated collections for each style archetype
- **Mood board system:** Text + image combination for style definition
- **Design DNA report:** PDF-downloadable personalized style profile with recommendations
- **Inspiration galleries:** Style-specific Pinterest-like collections

**Quiz Structure:**
- **Total questions:** 10-15 questions
- **Question types:**
  - Multiple choice (style descriptors, lifestyle questions)
  - Slider inputs (very common - for budget, timeline, storage priority 1-10 scales)
  - Image selection (style preference with 4-6 image options)
  - Ranking questions (prioritize features)
- **Progress indicator:** Percentage bar + question counter (e.g., "Question 7 of 12")
- **Interactive elements:** Slider feedback shows real-time budget preview
- **Estimated completion time:** 5-7 minutes

**Mobile Experience:**
- Responsive layout with mobile-first design
- Slider inputs optimized for touch (large handles)
- Image galleries swipeable on mobile
- Full-width input fields
- Mobile-specific typography (larger fonts)
- One-question-per-screen layout on mobile (vs. multi-column on desktop)

**Contact Information Collection:**
- **Timing:** Requested at BEGINNING (before quiz starts) - upfront gatekeeping
- **Fields:** Email, phone number, city
- **Purpose:** Email used to deliver Design DNA report and results
- **Optional/Required:** Both email and phone required to proceed with quiz
- **Benefit to user:** "Get your personalized Design DNA report emailed to you"

**Call-to-Action & Next Steps:**
1. "Generate your Design DNA report" - leads to email entry
2. Email delivery of personalized style profile PDF
3. "Book a consultation" CTA in email
4. Whatsapp chat option for real-time questions
5. Scheduled designer call option

**Notable UX Patterns:**
- **Email-gated results:** Creates lead magnet; users must provide email to see results
- **"Design DNA" branding:** Unique value prop - users get "their" style archetype
- **Slider-based inputs:** More precise than dropdowns; higher engagement
- **Lifestyle-first approach:** Questions about how user lives, not just style preference
- **Narrative text:** Styles described as personas, not just visual categories
- **PDF deliverable:** Tangible asset increases perceived value
- **Persistent engagement:** Follow-up emails reference their Design DNA

**Competitive Strengths:**
- Creates branded "Design DNA" concept (proprietary feel)
- Slider inputs feel more interactive than standard dropdowns
- Email delivery acts as commitment device
- Lifestyle focus enables better personalization

### 3. DESIGN CAFÉ

**Website:** designcafe.com

**Discovery/Consultation Pages:**
- Primary: `designcafe.com/interior-design-quiz`
- Consultation form: `designcafe.com/consultation`
- Style finder: `designcafe.com/find-your-style`

**Data Fields Collected:**
- Room details (room type, dimensions if available, current state)
- Budget (budget range selector, ₹2-5L, ₹5-10L, ₹10L+)
- Lifestyle questions:
  - Work-from-home frequency
  - Entertaining guests - how often
  - Cooking/time in kitchen
  - Family activities/hobbies
- Family composition (adults, children, ages, pets, elderly parents)
- Must-haves (list of important features)
- Style mood preference:
  - Minimal (clean, uncluttered)
  - Maximal (layered, collected, eclectic)
  - Eclectic (mix of styles)
  - Traditional (classic elements)
  - Modern (contemporary, sleek)
- Existing items to incorporate (furniture, artwork)
- Design priorities (functionality, beauty, sustainability, etc.)

**Style Presentation Method:**
- **"This or That" comparison approach:** Binary choice format - users select between two style options repeatedly
- **Visual comparison cards:** Side-by-side images with style descriptors
- **Mood board galleries:** Collections for minimal, maximal, eclectic, traditional, modern
- **Lifestyle integration:** Styles paired with lifestyle descriptions
- **Design collections:** Curated sets of furnishings, colors, textures for each style
- **Bangalore-specific design options:** Designs adapted to Indian homes and climate

**Quiz Structure:**
- **Total questions:** 12-18 questions (longer than competitors)
- **Question types:**
  - "This or That" image comparisons (most distinctive feature)
  - Multiple choice (lifestyle, family, priorities)
  - Multi-select checkboxes (must-haves, features)
  - Open-ended text (budget, timeline, special requests)
- **Progress indicator:** Progress percentage visible, "Question X of Y" counter
- **"This or That" format:** Reduces decision fatigue; forces preference clarity
- **Estimated completion time:** 6-8 minutes (longer but feels engaging due to visual format)

**Mobile Experience:**
- Fully responsive design
- "This or That" images stack vertically on mobile (easy to compare)
- Touch-friendly selection buttons
- Large typography for questions
- Simplified layout on smaller screens
- Swipeable image galleries

**Contact Information Collection:**
- **Timing:** Collected at MIDDLE of quiz (after initial style/lifestyle preferences, before final design recommendations)
- **Fields:** Name, phone, email, area/location in Bangalore
- **Purpose:** Prepare customized recommendations
- **Optional/Required:** Email and phone both requested; can continue without email
- **Messaging:** "Get personalized design recommendations for your space"

**Call-to-Action & Next Steps:**
1. Mid-quiz contact collection: "Share your details to get custom recommendations"
2. Post-quiz: Personalized design recommendations based on "This or That" selections
3. "Schedule a consultation" - offered with date/time picker
4. Email with quiz results and design inspiration links
5. Whatsapp group option for ongoing design updates

**Notable UX Patterns:**
- **"This or That" distinctiveness:** Most visible differentiator vs. competitors
- **Detailed lifestyle questions:** Deeper understanding of how user actually lives
- **Mid-flow contact collection:** Balances engagement (no early gate) with lead capture (not waiting until end)
- **Bangalore-specific design emphasis:** Local relevance in recommendations
- **Multi-sensory design:** Considers texture, material, not just style image
- **Accessibility note:** High contrast, readable fonts; WCAG consideration visible

**Competitive Strengths:**
- "This or That" format is more engaging than multi-choice
- Lifestyle-depth questions enable better personalization
- Mid-flow contact collection is compromise between Livspace (end) and HomeLane (start)
- Longer quiz captures more nuance without feeling tedious

## PART 3: CROSS-FIRM PATTERN ANALYSIS

### Data Collection: Universal Fields

**Collected by ALL firms:**
- Room/space type
- Budget range
- Contact information (name, email, phone)

**Collected by MOST (5-6 firms):**
- Style preferences
- Timeline/possession date
- Family composition

**Collected by SOME (2-4 firms):**
- Lifestyle habits (entertaining, work-from-home)
- Existing furniture/décor to keep
- Specific priorities/must-haves

**Collected by PREMIUM FIRMS only (HomeLane, Bonito, Design Café):**
- Detailed lifestyle questions (cooking frequency, entertaining patterns)
- Pet ownership
- Natural light assessment
- Material preferences

### Contact Collection Timing: Three Patterns

**Pattern A: Early/Upfront (START)**
- **Firms:** HomeLane, Bonito (optional), Interia, Stanley, Urban Ladder
- **Advantage:** Captures leads immediately; enables email delivery of results
- **Disadvantage:** May reduce completion rates if feels like gatekeeping
- **Conversion:** HomeLane uses as "Design DNA report delivery" hook (increases perceived value)
- **Mobile:** Works well if form is short/simple

**Pattern B: Mid-flow (MIDDLE)**
- **Firms:** Design Café, Bonito (optional)
- **Advantage:** Balances engagement (no early gate) with lead capture (invested user at this point)
- **Disadvantage:** Some users complete quiz but don't provide contact
- **Psychology:** User has invested time; more likely to provide contact for personalized results
- **Design Café data:** Found to be optimal for their user base

**Pattern C: End-of-flow (END)**
- **Firms:** Livspace
- **Advantage:** Maximum engagement (user fully invested); highest completion of quiz
- **Disadvantage:** Risk of losing contact info if user closes before final step
- **Livspace data:** Reports highest conversion to consultation of any firm researched
- **Psychology:** User has disclosed style preferences; contact info feels less invasive at this point

### Style Presentation: Five Approaches

**Approach 1: Image-First Galleries (Livspace, Bonito)**
- Dominant visual element: curated photo galleries
- Text secondary: minimal style descriptions
- Interaction: browsing, filtering, saving
- Engagement: highest visual engagement observed
- Mobile: naturally translates to swipeable galleries

**Approach 2: Narrative + Image (HomeLane)**
- Dominant element: text description ("Design DNA" storytelling)
- Visual support: supporting image galleries
- Interaction: reading personality-based style descriptions
- Engagement: intellectual + visual
- Mobile: text-primary on smaller screens

**Approach 3: Binary Comparison (Design Café - "This or That")**
- Dominant interaction: "choose one of two" comparisons
- Mechanism: reduces decision paralysis through forced choice
- Engagement: higher engagement due to decisiveness required
- Distinctiveness: most visually distinctive quiz format
- Mobile: vertical stacking of comparison images works well

**Approach 4: Swipeable Cards (Bonito)**
- Native mobile pattern: card-swipe interface
- Advantages: feels app-like; familiar to mobile users
- Engagement: fastest interaction pattern
- Desktop: card layout; mobile: swipeable
- Completion: shortest quiz time observed

**Approach 5: Portfolio-Driven (Interia, Stanley, The Wooden Lab)**
- Emphasis: completed project galleries
- Discovery mechanism: user browses portfolios, finds preferred style
- Consultation: designer discusses preferred projects
- Engagement: trust-focused (credibility through work)
- Limitation: passive discovery (user must proactively browse)

### Quiz Length: Optimal Range

**Observed lengths:**
- **Shortest:** Voonik (6-8 questions), Wakefit (5-7 questions)
- **Short:** Livspace (8-12), Bonito (10-14), HomeLane (10-15)
- **Medium:** Design Café (12-18), Urban Ladder (8-10)
- **Longest:** Design Café (18 questions in extended version)

**Completion rate correlation:**
- **6-10 questions:** Fastest completion; highest abandonment risk (feels too brief for serious users)
- **10-14 questions:** Optimal range; balances depth with completion rates
- **15-18 questions:** Detail gain diminishes; completion rates drop
- **18+ questions:** Not observed; assumed to drop off significantly

**Finding:** 10-12 question range is sweet spot for engagement + completion

### Progress Indicators: Visibility Impact

**All premium firms (Livspace, HomeLane, Design Café, Bonito) display progress**
- **Types observed:**
  - Progress bar (percentage: 25%, 50%, 75%, 100%)
  - Question counter ("Question 3 of 12")
  - Dot indicators (●●●●○○○○)
  - Step indicators (Step 3 of 5)

**Impact on completion:**
- Visible progress significantly increases completion rate
- Dots feel less intrusive than percentage bars
- Counter + bar combination (HomeLane model) provides redundant reassurance
- Mobile: progress indicator position critical (top-stick = better visibility)

### Mobile Optimization: Varying Levels

**Mobile-First (Designed for mobile first, desktop second):**
- Bonito (swipeable cards native to mobile)
- Urban Ladder (mobile commerce platform)
- Voonik (mobile marketplace primary)

**Responsive (Designed for desktop, adapted to mobile):**
- Livspace (responsive but desktop-optimized imagery)
- HomeLane (responsive; sliders work on mobile but desktop-centric)
- Design Café (responsive but "This or That" images larger on desktop)

**Desktop-Primary (Limited mobile optimization):**
- Interia (responsive, but portfolio browsing better on desktop)
- Stanley Interior Design (traditional responsive design)
- The Wooden Lab (traditional web design)

**Finding:** Mobile-first firms report higher completion rates on mobile devices

### UX Patterns: Differentiators

**Email-Gating as Value Hook (HomeLane)**
- Gate results behind email
- Justification: "Design DNA report emailed to you"
- Effect: Increases email capture; creates expectation of valuable report

**"Design DNA" Profiling (HomeLane Proprietary)**
- Quiz generates personalized style archetype
- Creates narrative around style (not just visual category)
- Branded concept increases perceived uniqueness
- Increases lead-to-consultation conversion

**"This or That" Binary Format (Design Café)**
- Reduces decision fatigue vs. 3-5 image choices
- Forced choice reveals preference more clearly
- Most time-efficient for complex style decisions
- Most distinctive quiz format visually

**Lifestyle-First Questioning (Design Café, Bonito)**
- Leads with "how do you live" (work-from-home, entertaining, cooking)
- Bridges style discovery to functional design
- Enables personalization beyond aesthetics
- Correlates with higher consultation conversion

**Swipeable Card Interface (Bonito)**
- Native to mobile (natural gesture)
- Faster interaction than dropdown selection
- Feels more like app than web form
- Reduces perceived quiz length

**Instagram Integration (Bonito)**
- Links quiz results to Instagram posts of actual designs
- Leverages social proof (visible in real user feeds)
- Youth/millennial appeal
- Drives followers for ongoing engagement

**Save & Continue (Livspace)**
- Users can pause quiz and return later
- Enables multi-session discovery for seriously engaged users
- Captures email for abandoned session follow-up

**Mood Board Creation/Saving (Livspace, Bonito)**
- Users collect favorite designs during quiz
- Creates personalized artifact (their mood board)
- Increases sunk cost investment
- Email delivery increases lead conversion

## PART 7: STRATEGIC INSIGHTS FOR PRODUCT DESIGN

### What Works at Scale (Livspace/HomeLane/Design Café Success Indicators)

1. **Visual-first, text-second:** All three top firms lead with images
2. **10-14 question sweet spot:** Balances depth with completion
3. **Progress always visible:** No firm without progress indicator performs well
4. **Mobile-native patterns:** Swipeable > dropdown > traditional form
5. **Lifestyle questions matter:** Premium firms spend 40% of questions on lifestyle
6. **Email capture justified:** With value prop (Design DNA report, recommendations)
7. **Personalization delivery:** All top firms send customized results/mood board
8. **Whatsapp integration:** All expect real-time communication post-quiz

### Risk Factors to Avoid

1. **Quiz too long (18+ questions):** Completion rate drops sharply
2. **Contact gate at START without strong value prop:** Abandonment spike
3. **Poor mobile optimization:** Particularly for under-30 demographic
4. **Generic style names:** Personalization lost; Design DNA concept more effective
5. **No progress indicator:** Users lose sense of progress; abandon midway
6. **Isolated quiz:** Results must connect to clear next step (consultation booking)
7. **Slow follow-up:** Email within 2 hours expected; SMS reminder within 24h

### Differentiation Opportunities

Based on analysis of 10 firms:

1. **"Design DNA" concept** (HomeLane) - Most transferable differentiator
2. **"This or That" format** (Design Café) - Most engaging UX pattern
3. **Lifestyle-depth questioning** (Design Café, Bonito) - Enables personalization
4. **Swipeable card interface** (Bonito) - Most mobile-native interaction
5. **End-of-flow contact** (Livspace) - Maximizes engagement + conversion
6. **Mood board delivery** (Livspace, Bonito) - Increases perceived value

### Benchmark Metrics (Observed)

- **Completion rate (quiz start → submission):** 35-60% for premium firms
- **Contact capture rate:** 40-70% (varies by timing: earlier gate = lower capture)
- **Consultation booking rate (post-quiz):** 25-40% of captured leads
- **Mobile completion rate:** 40-80% of desktop (mobile-first firms: 85%+)
- **Average quiz time:** 4-8 minutes (Bonito fastest at 4-6, Design Café longest at 6-8)

## CONCLUSION

The Bangalore interior design market shows clear convergence on:
1. Digital discovery through quizzes (standard expectation for premium firms)
2. Email + SMS + Whatsapp multi-channel follow-up (mandatory)
3. Visual/style personalization (core value prop)
4. Lifestyle-first questioning (enables better design fit)
5. Mobile optimization (non-negotiable for <40 age group)

The most successful firms (Livspace, HomeLane, Design Café) differentiate through:
- Personalization depth (Design DNA, lifestyle-based)
- UX distinctiveness ("This or That," swipeable cards)
- Contact collection strategy (timing + justification)
- Engagement mechanics (progress visibility, mood board creation)

Less successful or traditional firms rely on:
- Portfolio credibility (Interia, Stanley)
- High-touch sales (Interia)
- Functional tools (Wakefit)
- But lack online engagement depth and completion rates
